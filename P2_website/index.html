New York City Park Trails in designated 'Forever Wild' areas.

NYC Parks' "Forever Wild" initiative protects over 500 acres of unique, ecologically valuable sites across all five boroughs.

This analysis is based on datasets provided by NYC Open Data: Parks Trails Dataset

Data includes information about park where trail is located, width of trail, surface type of trail, difficulty rating, rank of amount developed, and date of information collected. Limitations include width values rounded to the nearest foot.

Hypothesis:

  • Undeveloped trails tend to have a smaller average width, and are more likely to have natural surfaces like dirt.
  • Developed trails tend to have a greater average width and are more likely to have unnatural surfaces such as pavement or concrete.
  • There is a relationship between trail surface type and difficulty rating. Trails with paved or unnatural surfaces are associated with lower difficulty levels.

Analysis:

    I wanted to see if there was any relationship between development of trails, dificulty, and surface type.
  • I found that there are more observations of developed trails overall.
  • The most frequent difficulty of trail is a level 2, or slightly uneven.
  • Most paths are either dirt or paved, with an average of around 7 feet in width.
  • I was not able to find an obvious relationship between year of data collected and other variables.
